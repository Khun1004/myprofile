I was born and raised in Myanmar with no prior exposure to coding or computers — just general academic study. Coming to Korea grew out of a spirit of adventure and openness to new opportunities. During a year at a Korean language institute, I was exposed to a range of technical fields, and found myself drawn to designing and building websites in particular — which naturally led me toward frontend development, where I could shape the visual and interactive layer of a product myself.

I then studied web development systematically from the ground up in the Software / AI Big Data program at Yeungjin College. Everything felt unfamiliar at first, but understanding HTML's structure and styling it with CSS gave me a clear sense of accomplishment. Using JavaScript and React to bring static pages to life — building dynamic UI that responds to the user — is where I fell into the depth and possibility of frontend development. Adjusting details like layout balance, color harmony, and typographic readability made finishing a page feel like completing a small piece of craft.

I also gained mobile interface design experience through an Android app development course. Placing information effectively on a small screen and designing user flow around touch interaction has its own charm, distinct from the web — and that experience became a solid foundation for adapting flexibly across devices and platforms. Facing unfamiliar environments and technologies with curiosity rather than fear, building skill step by step, is the mindset that keeps me learning and adapting in this fast-moving field.

"Every line of code should carry care for the user" — a philosophy I arrived at through building the PongPongPuppy project.

Focus & Diligence

I had never touched programming before coming to Korea, but once something catches my interest, I dig into it on my own. Tools like React and Git weren't covered in my formal coursework, but when a team project needed them, I taught myself through online resources and official docs — which let me build React-based websites and even React Native apps. I lose track of time getting hands-on with a technology and seeing a result appear on screen, sometimes forgetting to eat while I'm deep in it. That focus and diligence is my greatest strength as a developer.

Multilingual, Cross-Cultural Sense

Growing up in Myanmar and adapting to a new environment in Korea, I now speak Korean fluently at Level 5. I can also communicate in English at an intermediate level, and I'm steadily building Japanese and Thai from the basics. Learning and using multiple languages has given me a foundation for communicating smoothly with people from different cultural backgrounds — a real asset when designing user-centered services or collaborating with a team. I'm confident this language sense and cultural understanding would be especially valuable at a company with a global footprint.

Growth Area — Refined Through Collaboration

I tend to worry excessively about inconveniencing others. Afraid of making mistakes or being a burden, I often tried to handle everything on my own. Through repeated team projects, though, I've come to appreciate the value of working together, and I'm learning to share difficulties openly and ask for help when needed. I can now voice my own opinions and negotiate while still keeping the team's harmony in mind, and my collaboration skills keep improving. Building on my focus and self-directed learning, I want to keep working through this growth area through collaboration and communication, becoming a developer who has a positive impact on the team.

Career Aspirations

"A user-focused frontend developer, designing experiences that get the details right."

I believe the core of frontend development isn't just building features — it's shaping how users feel and behave through technology. Building the PongPongPuppy project, I felt firsthand how much a single pixel of CSS, a color choice, or the use of whitespace can shape the entire user experience. Through the New You project, I built the ability to bring complex features together intuitively on mobile and design smooth user flows. I want to work somewhere I can add real value through careful design sense and strong technical execution.

1

A Developer Who Follows Through

I don't stop at making a feature work — I think through how the result affects real users and push for polish. I'll catch every bug and every design inconsistency, checking things through to the end from the user's point of view.

2

A Colleague Who Grows With the Team

I value the team's growth over individual credit. I won't insist on my own opinion over the team's direction, and I'll share knowledge openly to help build a culture where we learn and grow together.

3

An Expert Who Turns Learning Into Practice

In a fast-changing IT landscape, I won't slack on learning new tools and technologies — and I'll prove that learning through real gains in productivity and usability.

Short-Term Goals

After joining, my first priority is adapting quickly to the team's development culture and process. I'll learn to see my code through a teammate's eyes via code review, and help maintain code consistency and quality with tools like ESLint and Prettier. My first goal is to understand the design system thoroughly and carefully build reusable components that boost the team's development productivity. I'll also actively learn CI/CD, which I haven't yet applied in a production setting, so I can get up to speed on the deployment process quickly.

Long-Term Goals

Long term, I want to grow beyond implementation into a proactive developer who analyzes user data and feedback to identify business problems and propose solutions. Drawing on experience across web and mobile, I want to help deliver a consistent UX across platforms, and take the lead on frontend architecture improvements that raise both development productivity and user satisfaction. My goal is to become a colleague who sparks technical discussion within the team and helps build a better development culture.

The adventurous spirit and adaptability I built through learning Korean and adjusting to a new culture are my greatest assets. I'll stay open to learning and flexible in the face of new technologies and changing needs, always keeping the user first, and do my best to be a colleague people can rely on.
